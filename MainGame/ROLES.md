# Jobs & Roles Plan

Inventory of jobs and roles that need to populate the world described in [MAP.md](MAP.md), filtered through the NPC archetypes in [NPCs.md](NPCs.md). The point of this doc is to make sure every location described in the map has at least one named role attached to it — and that cross-cutting town services (mail, police, garbage, etc.) aren't forgotten just because they don't live in a single building.

Conventions used below:

- **Role** — a job title or social position. Multiple NPCs may fill the same role over the 6-year arc.
- **Archetype** — best-fit NPCs.md category (Senior / Adult / Young Adult / HS / MS / Grade / Toddler / Nun / Monk / Priest).
- **Count** — rough head count needed for the location to feel populated. Single number means one role-holder; "1–2" means rotating.
- **Notes** — any text from MAP.md / NPCs.md that pins this person down (personality hooks, schedule, quirks).

## 13. Aging & continuity

NPCs.md sets up a 6-year arc (Grade 4-ish → end of MS). The roles above need to age forward:

- **Same role, new face**: lifeguard, fast-food cashier, paperboy, babysitter — HS jobs naturally turn over as players age in. Author 2–3 successive NPCs per role.
- **Career ladders**: today's HS busser becomes a Young Adult diner waitress; today's Young Adult repair-tech becomes the Adult electronics-repair shopkeep. Map a couple of these explicitly so the player notices.
- **Retirement / death**: Senior roles (head priest, mayor, head librarian, pinsetter mechanic) should turn over once across the arc. The cemetery gets one new occupant the player knew.
- **Move-in / move-out**: One neighbor home should empty and refill. New family is a new social hub.
- **Marriage / kids**: One Young Adult couple has a baby; one Adult kid graduates and leaves for college. Mark which NPCs anchor these beats.
- **Promotion**: Assistant priest → head priest possible if you want the head priest to retire mid-arc.

## 14. Implementation suggestions (where to start)

A staged path so you don't have to author 200 NPCs before any scene feels populated:

1. **Tier 1 — Player's daily life**: Family, 3 neighbors, school staff (1 teacher + janitor + bus driver), one shopkeeper (toy or comic store), the priest, one nun.
2. **Tier 2 — Weekly stops**: Diner waitress, donut clerk, gas-station cashier, library staff, arcade cashier, movie theater concessions kid, fast-food crew.
3. **Tier 3 — Occasional/specialty**: Doctor, dentist, electronics-repair shopkeep, museum curator, bait-shop owner, train station master, fairground info booth.
4. **Tier 4 — Cameos & background**: Mail carrier, cops, fishermen, dirt-track regulars, farm hands, town wino, repair-tech, dog walker. These can be anonymous walking sprites with minimal dialogue.
5. **Tier 5 — Optional sites**: Halfway house, orphanage, retirement home if they make the cut from §12.

## 15. Open questions

- **Halfway house / orphanage / retirement home**: do these get their own areas in MAP.md, or stay implied? See §12.
- **Police / fire / EMS station**: not currently on the area grid — does the town actually have one, or is service implied (consolidated in town hall, or piggy-backing on the fairground first-aid)?
- **Where do farm kids come from**: NPCs.md mentions they're bussed in. Do specific farms map to specific kid NPCs, or are they treated as a homogenous group?
- **Player gender / family makeup**: not specified. Affects who the player's parents are and which neighbors plausibly babysit. Probably worth pinning down before naming Tier-1 NPCs.
- **Named-vs-anonymous threshold**: how many of the ~150 roles above need a real name and dialogue, vs. a generic walk-cycle? A reasonable target might be 30–50 named, the rest as flavor crowd.
- **Nun ninjas**: the convent grounds description hints at this gag. Is it canon? Affects whether nuns ever get a "stealthy appearance" mechanic.
